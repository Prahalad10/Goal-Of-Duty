Death Stranding

“Once, there was an explosion…”

Kojima’s Death Stranding is bold, bizarre, and often brilliant. It's a game about isolation, reconnection, and carrying burdens — sometimes literal, sometimes emotional.

You are Sam Porter Bridges, a lone courier in a broken world. What starts as simple delivery work slowly evolves into a deeply philosophical journey across haunting landscapes and between fragmented souls.

With atmospheric sound design, weighty traversal, and haunting narrative moments, it’s not for everyone — but for those it *is* for, it’s unforgettable.

“Tomorrow is in your hands.”

Score: 9/10
